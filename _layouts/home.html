About

I am currently Juniorprofessor at the University of Heidelberg. Before moving to Heidelberg in April 2020, I was a postdoc at MIT in the group of Youssef Marzouk. In 2018 I completed my PhD at ETH Zürich with Christoph Schwab on Sparse-Grid Approximation of High-Dimensional Parametric PDEs. In my free time, I enjoy reading books on history, taking pictures, listening to music and going to museums.

Research

My research focuses on the methodological foundations and theory of forward and inverse problems in Uncertainty Quantification. I am interested in developing and analyzing algorithms for high-dimensional approximation based on sparse-grid techniques, neural networks and transport methods.
